Self-Containment
[
]
A Box is self-contained with a structure of
Metoms
. It is recursively contained by an instance of itself with an identical definition by which objects are permanently bound by. Objects with alternative existence ranks and infinite speed are not bound by a Box.
A Box's self-containment can be escaped by having an existence rank outside of the existence and non-existence binary; the Boxial Core's fluids do not interact with non-binary states.
Formation of our local Box
[
]
Main article:
Omniverse
The Box local to Earth was initialized as an infinite recursive empty structure. It possessed instability that collapsed Boxial layers. Eventually, it exploded, bringing entropy that would lead to a convergence towards its current state.

[
]
The Box's current structure is an infinite-dimensional hyperstructure that contains bubbles and recursive copies of itself. These layers iterate infinitely. Within the structure's voids, minor
