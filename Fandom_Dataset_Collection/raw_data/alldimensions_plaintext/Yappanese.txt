[
]

👍 is the letter for θ
👍can be θ or ð depending on which is easier to say in the context of the word
ʃ is written as 🤩
ʔ is written as 🤐 and is used to separate vowels (predominantly used in verbs)
' means contraction
c is used before a, o and u and q is used before e and i. If c is used before e or i then it is pronounced /s/ instead of /k/.
s inbetween two vowels is pronounced as z; you can prevent this by using ss instead of one s (Example: plasa is pronounced /plaza/ but zassei🤩e is pronounced /zasɛiʃɛ/

Rules
[
]
All verbs end in -re (Example: sere - to be)
All additional info is in optional suffixes
Person Information is just pronouns as suffixes (if pronoun starts in vowel then add a 🤐), for example Sere🤐ere means I am, I was, I am going to be, etc. (no time was specified) and comes first
Time information:
-nau (Present)
-uas (Past)
-uil (Future)
-ia (Continuous, can add to any time info except present)
You can make a verb with time info and person info but also you can make verbs with only time info, for example serenau🤐ia meaning "somebody is being" where that somebody can be me, you, etc and is unspecified. Can be used if the person is unknown.
Example - seretu🤐uil means "you will be" and sere🤐eluasia means "he/she was being"
If the subject is a pronoun (example: i am, he ate, it took) then don't put it behind the verb, just use the person suffix. If you're referring to a specified thing (example: the thing exploded, my underwear is stinky, etc) then put it behind the verb

Imperative
[
]
Imperative sentences are sentences that communicate orders / commands (example: go take out the trash!). In Yappanese, imperative sentences are structured like this:
[pronoun] [verb] [noun]
In imperative sentences, verbs have the prefix go-- or g'-.
Example: Tu go-xere para mi caru. (Go to my car)
Questions
[
]
Question sentences always start with question words:
wala - what
wape - who
wani - where
wamo - when
wasi - how
waxa - how many
wa🤩o - did (as in "Did they really do that?")
Example: Wala sere🤐el mage👍e? (What is magnesium?)
